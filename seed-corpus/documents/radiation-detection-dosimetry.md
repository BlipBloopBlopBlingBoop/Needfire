# Radiation Detection and Dose

Radiation is invisible; managing it means measuring it and controlling three
things — **time, distance, and shielding**. This complements *Radiation Basics and
Shielding*, focusing on detection, dose, and how fallout fades.

## Measuring it
- **Geiger–Müller counter / survey meter:** measures a **dose rate** now (e.g.
  µSv/h or mR/h). Good for finding hotspots and checking a shelter, food, or
  water for contamination.
- **Dosimeter:** measures **accumulated dose** over time — what your body has
  actually received. A personal dosimeter (electronic, film badge, or an RADIACs
  pen) tells you your running total, which is what matters for health.
- Rate × time = accumulated dose. Keep the running total low.

## Dose and health (rough guide)
Dose is measured in **sieverts (Sv)**; 1 Sv = 1000 mSv. Natural background is a
few mSv per **year**. Short, whole-body doses:
- **< ~100 mSv:** no immediate illness; small long-term risk.
- **~1 Sv (1000 mSv):** radiation sickness — nausea, vomiting, fatigue.
- **~4–5 Sv:** often fatal without advanced medical care.
- **> ~6 Sv:** usually fatal.
The aim in a fallout situation is to **keep your accumulated dose as low as
possible** — every reduction in rate or exposure time helps.

## The three protections
- **Time:** minimise time in a radiation field; dose is rate × time.
- **Distance:** intensity falls with the **square of distance** — double the
  distance, one-quarter the dose.
- **Shielding:** mass between you and the source. Each **"half-value thickness"**
  cuts the dose rate in half — roughly **1 cm of lead, ~6 cm of concrete, ~9 cm of
  packed earth, or ~12 cm of water** for typical fallout gamma. Stack halvings:
  two half-values = ¼, ten = ~1/1000.

## Fallout fades fast — the 7-10 rule
Fresh fallout is intensely radioactive but decays quickly: **for every 7-fold
increase in time, the dose rate drops about 10-fold.**
- At **7 hours** after the blast, the rate is ~**1/10** of the 1-hour rate.
- At **49 hours (~2 days)**, ~**1/100**.
- At **~2 weeks**, ~**1/1000**.
So the crucial move is to **shelter through the first hours to days** behind mass;
the danger drops dramatically with time. Come out briefly only for essential tasks,
minimising time and maximising distance and shielding.

## Contamination vs. exposure
Fallout is dust you can brush off — **remove outer clothing and wash exposed skin
and hair** on entering shelter to cut contamination. Cover water and food; filter
or wipe surfaces. Being *near* radioactive dust is the hazard, so keeping it off
you and out of your shelter is protection you can act on.
