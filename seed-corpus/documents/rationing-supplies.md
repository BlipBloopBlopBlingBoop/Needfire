# Rationing Food and Water

When resupply is uncertain, knowing how long your stores last — and how to
stretch them without wrecking your body — turns panic into a plan. Water is
almost always the tighter constraint: you survive weeks without food but only
days without water.

## Water — the hard limit
Plan on **about 3 litres per person per day** for drinking and food preparation
in a temperate climate at rest, and store more:
- **4 litres (1 gallon) per person per day** is the common preparedness planning
  figure, covering some washing and cooking.
- **Double it** in heat, at altitude, for hard work, or for pregnant, nursing,
  sick, or injured people.
The **survival floor** is roughly **2 litres per person per day** of drinking
water; below that, thinking and stamina degrade fast and kidney damage follows.
**Never ration drinking water below need to "save" it** — drink what your body
requires today and put your effort into finding and purifying more. Ration
*sweat*, not water: work in the cool hours, rest in shade.

## Food — you have more slack
An adult needs roughly **2,000 kcal per day** to maintain weight and full
function. In a genuine shortage the body tolerates far less for a while:
- **1,200–1,500 kcal/day** is a sustainable short-term ration that preserves
  strength and clear thinking for weeks.
- Below ~1,200 kcal/day you will lose weight and capacity steadily; reserve
  deep cuts for true scarcity and keep the ration highest for those doing the
  hardest work, and for children and the pregnant or nursing.
Do not starve yourself to "stretch" food while doing heavy labour — match intake
to the work, or reduce the work.

## Estimating how long stores last
- **Days of water = litres stored ÷ (people × litres per person per day).**
- **Days of food = total kcal stored ÷ (people × kcal per person per day).**
The toolkit's ration planner does this arithmetic for you and flags when a plan
drops below the survival floor.

## Stretching supplies
- **Find and purify water first** — it is renewable in most places; see *Water
  Purification Methods* and *Finding Water*.
- Cook dried staples (grains, beans) which swell severalfold in water.
- Keep morale food (something hot, something sweet); it matters more than its
  calories suggest.
- Track what you actually consume for a day or two, then set the ration from
  real numbers, not guesses.

> General reference only, not a substitute for medical or professional advice.
> Children, and pregnant, nursing, ill, or injured people have higher needs.
