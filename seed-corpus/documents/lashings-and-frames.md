# Lashings and Building Frames

Lashings bind poles together into structures — shelters, racks, tripods, litters,
rafts, and tables — with nothing but cordage. They are how you build when you have
no nails or brackets. Pair this with *Knots and Cordage*.

## Cordage and pole basics
Use strong cordage (natural rope, paracord inner strands, or plant cordage) and
straight, dry, sound poles. **Start and finish every lashing with a locking
knot** (a clove hitch or timber hitch) and pull each wrap tight — a loose lashing
racks and fails under load.

## The core lashings
- **Square lashing** — joins two poles that **cross at (or near) a right angle**
  and must not slide, e.g. a frame corner. Start with a clove hitch on one pole,
  wrap **around the cross three or more full turns** (inside the previous wraps on
  one pole, outside on the other), then make 2–3 **frapping turns** *between* the
  poles to cinch the wraps tight, and finish with a clove hitch.
- **Diagonal lashing** — for poles that cross but **tend to spring apart** (a
  diagonal brace). Start with a timber hitch around both poles pulled together,
  wrap across one diagonal three times, then the other diagonal three times, add
  frapping turns, finish with a clove hitch. Diagonal braces are what stop a
  square frame from collapsing into a parallelogram.
- **Shear/round lashing** — binds two (or more) parallel poles, e.g. to **extend
  a pole** or make the legs of an A-frame or **shear legs**. Wrap several turns
  around the pair with light frapping; leave it slightly loose if the poles must
  open into legs.

## Useful structures
- **Tripod** (shear lashing three poles near one end, then spread the feet): a
  stable base for a pot hanger, a water-filter stand, a hide/cache, or a hoist.
- **A-frame and rectangular frames**: square-lash the corners, then **add a
  diagonal brace** — an unbraced rectangle folds flat under side load.
- **Litter/stretcher**: two long side poles, shorter rungs square-lashed across,
  a bed of woven cordage or clothing (see moving a casualty in *Assessing a
  Casualty*).

## Safety
Test any structure with a gentle load before trusting weight to it. Re-tighten
lashings as cordage stretches and settles. A frame meant to hold a person must
be over-built; assume it will be shock-loaded.
