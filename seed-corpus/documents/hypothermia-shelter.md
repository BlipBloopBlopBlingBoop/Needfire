# Shelter and Hypothermia

In cold or wet conditions, shelter is often a higher priority than water or food.
Heat loss kills faster than hunger or thirst.

## Building emergency shelter
- Get out of wind and off cold/wet ground — ground contact steals body heat.
  Insulate underneath you with leaves, branches, or a pad at least as much as
  above you.
- Keep it small: a shelter only needs to fit your body so it traps your heat.
- Use a natural feature (rock overhang, fallen tree) plus a lean-to of branches
  and debris. A thick debris layer (0.5 m / 2 ft) sheds rain and insulates.
- Stay dry. Wet clothing loses most of its insulation; replace or dry it.

## Recognizing hypothermia
Early: shivering, cold/pale skin, clumsiness, slurred speech, poor judgement.
Worsening: shivering stops, confusion, drowsiness, slow weak pulse. Loss of
shivering is a danger sign, not improvement.

## Rewarming
- Move to shelter; remove wet clothing; insulate with dry layers and cover the
  head and neck.
- Give warm sweet drinks **only if fully alert** and able to swallow. No alcohol.
- Apply gentle warmth to the core (chest, neck, armpits, groin). Avoid rubbing
  limbs or sudden intense heat, which can be harmful in severe cases.
- Handle a severely hypothermic person gently and seek advanced care.
