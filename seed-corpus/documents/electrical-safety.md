# Electrical Safety

Electricity kills two ways: through the heart, and through fire. Both are
preventable with discipline.

## If someone is shocked
1. **Do not touch the victim while they may still be energized — you will
   become the second victim.** Kill the source first: breaker off, battery
   disconnected. If you cannot, move the conductor away with a dry,
   non-conductive object (dry wood, plastic).
2. Once clear, treat it as a cardiac emergency: check breathing, start CPR if
   needed, send for help.
3. Treat burns at the entry and exit points.
4. **Even a "small" shock can injure the heart** — keep the person at rest and
   watch them for hours afterward.

## Safe practice off-grid
- **Treat every circuit as live until you have proven it dead** with a meter or
  tester — then test the tester.
- Use the **one-hand rule** near anything live: keep the other hand in your
  pocket so current cannot cross your chest.
- **Fuse every battery positive**, close to the battery. An unfused battery
  line is a fire waiting for a short.
- Match wire gauge to current. **Undersized wire overheats and starts fires**
  long before anything trips.
- A 12 V system will not normally shock you through dry skin, but it **can burn
  you and start fires** —
  shorting a large battery with a wrench is instant welding. Remove rings and
  metal tools' loose contact.
- Mains voltage and **inverter output can kill**. Treat them with full respect.
- Keep water away from all electrical work; wet skin conducts far better.
- Ground metal enclosures and generator frames so a fault trips the fuse
  instead of energizing the case.

> General reference only, not a substitute for trained professional care. If you
> can reach professional help or a trained responder, do so.
