# Food Safety and Preservation

Without refrigeration, preserving food and avoiding spoilage protects both
nutrition and health.

## Preservation methods
- **Drying / dehydration** — removes moisture so microbes cannot grow. Slice thin,
  dry in sun, wind, or low heat until brittle (fruit, leathery for meat/jerky).
  Salt-curing meat before drying improves safety.
- **Salting** — heavy salt draws out water and inhibits spoilage (fish, meat).
- **Smoking** — combines drying with antimicrobial smoke compounds; cold or hot
  smoke over hardwood.
- **Fermentation** — controlled microbial activity preserves and adds nutrients
  (sauerkraut, kimchi: submerge vegetables in ~2% salt brine, keep anaerobic).
- **Root cellaring** — cool, humid, dark storage for roots, tubers, apples.

## Avoiding foodborne illness
- "When in doubt, throw it out." Off smells, sliminess, mould (except on
  intentionally cured items), or swollen sealed containers signal danger.
- **Botulism** is a deadly risk in improperly preserved low-acid foods and in
  bulging cans — do not taste-test suspect canned goods. Boiling suspect food for
  10 minutes can destroy the toxin but not all spores; prevention is better.
- Cook meat thoroughly. Keep raw and cooked foods separate. Wash hands and tools.

## Foraging caution
Never eat a wild plant or mushroom unless you can positively identify it as safe.
Many edible species have toxic look-alikes. Misidentification can be fatal.
