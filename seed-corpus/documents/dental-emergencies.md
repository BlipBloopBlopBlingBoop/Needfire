# Dental Emergencies

Dental problems rarely kill quickly, but untreated infection can. Off-grid,
prevention and early action matter most.

## Toothache
Rinse with warm salt water and floss gently to remove trapped debris — a lodged
food particle is a common cause. A drop of clove oil on a small piece of cotton
placed against the tooth numbs pain. Cold packs on the cheek help swelling.

## Knocked-out permanent tooth
Time is everything — a tooth reinserted within minutes can survive.
1. **Handle it by the crown only; never touch the root.**
2. If dirty, rinse gently in clean water — do not scrub or remove tissue.
3. Push it back into its socket facing the right way and have the person bite
   gently on cloth to hold it.
4. If reinsertion is impossible, store the tooth in milk or in the person's own
   saliva (inside the cheek if they are alert). Do not let it dry out.
Baby teeth are not reinserted.

## Broken or chipped tooth
Save any fragments. Rinse the mouth, and cover a sharp edge or exposed area with
temporary filling material or softened wax to protect the tongue and nerve.

## Lost filling or crown
Clean the cavity gently and plug it with temporary filling material or softened
sugar-free wax; a crown can be reseated with temporary dental cement.

## Abscess — the dangerous one
A swollen, throbbing gum or face with fever means infection. **Swelling that
spreads to the jaw, neck, or eye, or trouble swallowing or breathing, can become
life-threatening.** Rinse frequently with warm salt water; an abscess sometimes
drains on its own, which relieves pressure. Definitive care is antibiotics and
drainage or extraction.

Off-grid, daily brushing and clean teeth prevent nearly all of the above.
