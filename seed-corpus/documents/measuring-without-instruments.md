# Measuring and Estimating Without Instruments

With no tape, rangefinder, or watch you can still measure well enough to build,
navigate, and plan — using your body, the sun, and a little arithmetic. Calibrate
the "body ruler" figures below against a known length once; everyone's are a
little different.

## Your body as a ruler (adult averages)
- **Thumb width** ≈ 2 cm; **palm width** (four fingers) ≈ 8–10 cm.
- **Hand span** (spread thumb to little fingertip) ≈ 20 cm.
- **Cubit** (elbow to fingertip) ≈ 45 cm.
- **Arm span / fathom** (fingertip to fingertip, arms wide) ≈ your height ≈ 1.7 m.
- **Pace** (two steps, same foot twice) ≈ 1.5 m; most adults take **60–70 paces
  per 100 m** — see *Navigation with Map and Compass* to calibrate and use it.

## Estimating height by shadows
On sunny ground, a tall object and a known object cast shadows in the same ratio:

    object height = (your height × object's shadow length) ÷ your shadow length

Stand beside the tree or pole, measure both shadows in paces or spans, and solve.
A stick of known length works in place of your body.

## Estimating distance
- **Thumb-jump (stadimetry).** Hold your thumb up at arm's length; close one eye,
  line the thumb on the target, then switch eyes. The thumb appears to jump
  sideways. Estimate how wide that jump is **at the target** (against a fence,
  field, or building of guessable size) and **multiply by 10** — that is roughly
  the distance to the target. (Arm length is about 10× the gap between your
  eyes.)
- **Appearance clues.** You can make out limbs on a person at ~1 km, faces at
  ~100 m — rough, and worse in haze or poor light.

## Estimating daylight left
Hold your hand at arm's length with the bottom finger resting on the horizon and
the sun above. **Each finger width between the sun and the horizon ≈ 15 minutes**
of daylight (four fingers ≈ 1 hour). Stack hands to count more. A clenched fist
spans about 10° of sky.

## Handy conversions
- Temperature: **°F = °C × 9/5 + 32**; **°C = (°F − 32) × 5/9**.
- Length: **1 m ≈ 3.28 ft**; **1 km ≈ 0.62 mi**; **1 inch = 2.54 cm**.
- Mass: **1 kg ≈ 2.2 lb**. Volume: **1 litre ≈ 0.26 US gal ≈ 0.22 imp gal**.
