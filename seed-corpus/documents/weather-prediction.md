# Reading the Weather

Without a forecast you can still read the sky and wind hours ahead — long enough
to shelter, secure gear, or change plans. Watch for **change and trend**, not a
single sign.

## Pressure — the master signal
Falling air pressure means worsening weather (wind, cloud, rain); rising pressure
means improving, settling weather.
- **Fast fall = a fast-moving storm.** The quicker the drop, the stronger the wind.
- No barometer? Watch the proxies below, and note that **distant sounds carry
  farther, smoke hangs low, and joints/old wounds ache** as pressure falls.

## Clouds — what the sky is doing
- **High, wispy cirrus ("mares' tails") thickening and lowering** into a milky
  sheet (cirrostratus, often with a halo round sun or moon) → a warm front and
  rain within ~12–24 hours.
- **Clouds lowering and thickening**, the sky greying over → rain approaching.
- **Towering cauliflower cumulus building tall by afternoon** → thunderstorms;
  an anvil top means a mature storm — take lightning seriously (*Lightning and
  Severe-Storm Safety*).
- **Small, fair-weather cumulus that flatten toward evening** → settled weather.
- **Clear, calm, dry nights** cool fast → dew or frost by morning.

## Wind and other signs
- **A shifting or backing wind and building gusts** signal a passing front and
  changing weather.
- **"Red sky at night, sailor's delight; red sky in morning, sailors take
  warning."** In the mid-latitudes (weather from the west): a red **sunset** lights
  clear air to the west → fair; a red **sunrise** lights clear air already passed
  east with cloud arriving from the west → worsening. A rough guide, not a law.
- **Dew or frost at dawn** suggests a dry, calm night just passed — often fair.
  A dry, humid, still morning can precede afternoon storms.
- **Animals and smoke:** birds roosting, insects low, and campfire smoke beaten
  down instead of rising can all mark falling pressure.

## Putting it together
Combine signs over a few hours: high cloud thickening and lowering, wind picking
up and shifting, pressure falling, halo around the moon → a front and rain are
coming; prepare shelter. Clearing skies, steady or rising pressure, and flattening
cloud → improving.

> General reference only. Local terrain and climate change these rules; mountains
> and coasts make their own weather. When storms threaten, err toward shelter.
