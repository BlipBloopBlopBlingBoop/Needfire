# Chemical Safety Basics

Most chemical injuries come from three mistakes: adding water to concentrated
acid, mixing cleaners, and skipping labels.

## Dilution — the golden rule
**Always add acid to water, and lye to water — never water to concentrated acid
or lye.** Dilution releases violent heat; a splash of water into concentrate
boils instantly and spits corrosive liquid at your face. Add slowly, stir, and
let heat disperse.

## Skin and eye contact
- **Flush skin with plain water for 15–20 minutes or more.** Remove
  contaminated clothing while flushing.
- **Do not try to "neutralize" chemicals on skin** — the reaction heat worsens
  the burn. Water only. Neutralize spills on floors and benches, never on
  people.
- Eyes: flush continuously with clean water for a full 20 minutes, lids held
  open.

## Never mix
**Never mix bleach with ammonia or with any acid.** These combinations release
chloramine or chlorine gas — a leading cause of home chemical deaths.
Ventilate whenever using either. **Warning signs of toxic gas: strong bleach
smell, a green-yellow tint to the air, burning eyes and throat — leave
immediately and ventilate before returning.**

## Storage and labeling
- Label every container with contents and date. **Never store chemicals in
  food or drink containers** — this is how children are poisoned.
- Store acids away from bases, and oxidizers (bleach, nitrates) away from
  fuels and organics.
- Keep containers closed, cool, and out of children's reach.

## Spills
Keep a small spill kit: absorbent (sand, dry soil, cat litter), soda ash or
baking soda for acid spills on floors, gloves, and a scoop. Absorb, neutralize
the surface if acid, collect, and dispose away from water sources.
