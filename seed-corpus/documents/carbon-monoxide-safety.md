# Carbon Monoxide — The Silent Killer

Carbon monoxide (CO) is a colourless, odourless gas made whenever fuel burns
without enough oxygen — charcoal, wood, propane, petrol, kerosene, natural gas.
Off-grid and disaster situations are exactly when people bring burning things
indoors, and CO poisoning is a leading cause of death after storms and power
cuts. **You cannot see, smell, or taste it.** It binds to blood far more tightly
than oxygen, so victims suffocate from the inside while the air looks fine.

## Never do these
- **Never run a generator, charcoal or gas grill, camp stove, or any petrol
  engine indoors** — not in a house, tent, garage, basement, or shed, and not
  near an open window or vent. A running generator needs to be **well away from
  and downwind of** any building, with exhaust pointed away.
- **Never use an oven, gas range, or outdoor heater to heat a living space.**
- **Never sleep in a vehicle with the engine running**, especially if snow or
  debris can block the exhaust pipe.

## Recognizing CO poisoning
Early signs mimic flu or food poisoning but **without a fever**:
- Headache, dizziness, nausea, weakness, confusion.
- **Everyone in the same space feels ill at once, and pets first.**
- Symptoms ease when you go outside and return indoors — a critical clue.
Worsening: vomiting, chest pain, loss of coordination, unconsciousness. Cherry-red
skin is a late and unreliable sign — do not wait for it.

## If you suspect CO
1. **Get everyone into fresh air immediately** — outdoors or by wide-open doors
   and windows.
2. Turn off the source if you can do it in seconds; otherwise just leave.
3. **Do a headcount and check anyone left inside.** Rescuers are also at risk —
   take a breath of fresh air first, be quick.
4. Seek medical help; CO can cause delayed brain and heart damage even after the
   person seems to recover. A pregnant person or an unconscious victim needs
   care urgently.

## Prevention
Use **battery-powered CO alarms** on every sleeping level and replace batteries
on a schedule. Keep flues, chimneys, and vents clear. Burn fuel only in
appliances designed and vented for it.

> reach professional help or a trained responder, do so.
