# Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS)

Dehydration from diarrhoea or vomiting is a leading cause of preventable death,
especially in children. A simple sugar-and-salt solution replaces lost water and
electrolytes and can be lifesaving.

## Standard home recipe
Mix into **1 litre of clean (purified) water**:
- **6 level teaspoons of sugar**
- **1/2 level teaspoon of salt**

Stir until dissolved. The solution should taste no saltier than tears. If it
tastes very salty, discard it and remake — too much salt is harmful, particularly
for small children.

## How to give it
- Give small, frequent sips — a spoonful every 1–2 minutes for a sick child.
- Continue after each loose stool or vomit. An adult may need 200–400 ml after
  each episode; a child, 50–100 ml.
- Keep offering it even if the person vomits; wait 10 minutes and resume slowly.
- Continue normal feeding/breastfeeding alongside ORS where possible.

## Zinc for children
For a child with diarrhoea, the WHO also recommends **zinc** alongside ORS: it
shortens the illness and makes the next episode less likely. Give **20 mg of
zinc once a day for 10–14 days** (10 mg a day for infants under 6 months), if
zinc tablets or syrup are available. Zinc is an add-on — the ORS is what saves
the life.

## Seek more help if
There is blood in the stool, a high fever, sunken eyes with no tears, no urine
for many hours, severe lethargy, or the person cannot keep any fluid down. These
signal severe dehydration that may need intravenous fluids.

> A balanced commercial ORS packet is preferable when available because the
> electrolyte ratios are precise. The home recipe is an emergency substitute.
