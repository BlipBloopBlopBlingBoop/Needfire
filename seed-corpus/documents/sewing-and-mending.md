# Sewing and Mending

Clothing and gear that stay repaired keep you warm, dry, and equipped. A needle,
thread, and a few stitches extend the life of everything you own. Keep a small
sewing kit in every pack.

## Kit and improvising
- **Needles:** a range of sizes; a thick "sailmaker" needle for heavy fabric,
  webbing, and leather. In a pinch, a needle can be improvised from wire, bone,
  or a thorn, and thread from unravelled cordage, dental floss, fishing line,
  sinew, or plant fibre.
- **Thread:** match strength to the job — strong thread for load-bearing seams,
  finer for cloth. Wax thread (beeswax) for grip and water resistance on heavy
  repairs.
- A **thimble** (or a coin) saves your fingers pushing through tough material.

## The essential stitches
- **Running stitch** — fast in-and-out stitch for light seams and tacking. Weak
  on its own.
- **Backstitch** — the workhorse **strong seam**: each stitch doubles back to
  meet the previous one, leaving a solid unbroken line on the underside. Use it
  for anything that takes strain.
- **Whipstitch / overcast** — loops over an edge to join two edges or stop fraying.
- **Darning** — for holes in knitwear/socks: weave a grid of threads across the
  hole (rows one way, then woven through the other way) to bridge it.

## Patching
1. Trim loose threads and round the corners of the tear (square corners tear
   onward).
2. Cut a patch of similar, sturdy fabric **larger than the hole** on all sides.
3. Turn the patch edges under and sew it down with a backstitch around the whole
   perimeter; for strength, patch the **inside** and topstitch from outside.
4. On tents, tarps, and rain gear, back the stitching with adhesive patch material
   or seal the seam so needle holes do not leak (see *Improvised Repairs and
   Adhesives*).

## Tips
- **Knot the thread end** and finish with several small stitches in place or a
  couple of half-hitches so the seam cannot unravel.
- Reinforce high-stress points (crotch, knees, pack straps, pocket corners) with
  a **bar-tack** (a bundle of short overlapping stitches) or a triangle of
  stitching.
- Sew before a small tear becomes a big one — a stitch in time genuinely saves
  nine.

> General reference only. Take care with needles and sharp tools, and keep
> your kit tidy and out of reach of children.
