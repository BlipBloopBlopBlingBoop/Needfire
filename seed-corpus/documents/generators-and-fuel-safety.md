# Generators and Fuel Safety

A generator bridges power gaps, but generators and their fuel kill people every
year through **carbon monoxide, fire, and electrocution**. Run them right and
store fuel safely.

## Carbon monoxide — the number-one killer
**Never run a generator indoors or in any enclosed or partly enclosed space** —
not a garage, shed, basement, or against an open window. Place it **well away from
and downwind of** buildings, with the exhaust pointed away from doors, windows,
and vents. CO is invisible and odourless — see *Carbon Monoxide — The Silent
Killer*. Keep battery CO alarms in living spaces.

## Electrical safety
- **Never "backfeed"** by plugging a generator into a wall outlet — it can
  electrocute utility workers and start fires, and bypasses your protection. Use a
  proper transfer switch or interlock, or run devices from the generator's own
  outlets with rated cords.
- Keep the generator and connections **dry**; operate under an open, ventilated
  cover in rain, never in standing water. Don't overload it — size the load to its
  rating.
- **Ground** the generator per its manual.

## Refuelling safely
- **Turn the engine OFF and let it cool** before refuelling — petrol spilled on a
  hot engine ignites. No smoking or flames nearby.
- Refuel outdoors, wipe spills, and keep a fire extinguisher or means to smother
  flames at hand.

## Storing fuel
- Use **approved, sealed fuel containers** (jerry cans), labelled by fuel type;
  never in food/drink containers.
- Store fuel **outdoors or in a detached, well-ventilated space**, away from
  living areas, heat, sparks, sunlight, and ignition sources (water heaters,
  pilots). Petrol vapour is heavier than air and travels to find a flame.
- **Petrol degrades in months**; **fuel stabiliser** extends it, and rotating your
  stock (use and refill) keeps it fresh. Diesel stores longer but can grow
  microbial "diesel bug" — use biocide and keep water out. Propane stores
  effectively indefinitely.
- Keep only what you can store safely and legally; large fuel stores are a serious
  fire load.

## Maintenance
Run the generator periodically under load, change oil and filters per the manual,
keep the air filter clean, and store it with the fuel system prepared for its
downtime. A generator that will not start when you need it is worthless.

## Safety
Fuel and engines combine fire, toxic exhaust, and high electrical energy. Keep
extinguishing means ready, never modify safety devices, and keep children and
bystanders clear of a running generator's exhaust and hot parts.
