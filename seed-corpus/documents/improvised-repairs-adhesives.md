# Improvised Repairs and Adhesives

A broken tool or leaking shelter can be as dangerous as an injury. Good repairs
buy time; great ones last. Carry a small repair kit and know what sticks to what.

## The repair kit that earns its weight
Duct/gaffer tape (rewound flat around a pencil to save space), a multi-tool,
cordage, a needle and strong thread, a few zip ties, safety pins, wire, a tube of
strong glue or epoxy, self-amalgamating tape, and adhesive fabric patches.

## Adhesives — pick the right one
- **Duct tape** — fast, waterproof-ish patches on tarps, packs, hoses, blisters,
  and splints. Sticks best to clean, dry, warm surfaces; press hard and round the
  corners so it does not peel.
- **Epoxy (two-part)** — the strongest general repair; bonds metal, wood, ceramic,
  most plastics, and fills gaps. Mix equal parts, work fast, clamp until set.
- **Cyanoacrylate ("super glue")** — instant bonds on small, tight, clean joints;
  brittle under shock and poor at filling gaps. Bonds skin instantly — take care.
- **Contact cement** — for gluing large flat areas and rubber/leather: coat both
  faces, let tack, then press.
- **Self-amalgamating (silicone) tape** — fuses to itself into a waterproof,
  pressure-resistant sleeve for hoses, leaks, and cable — no adhesive residue.
- **Improvised:** pine pitch/resin melted with a little charcoal and (optionally)
  fat makes **pitch glue**, a traditional waterproof adhesive for hafting and
  sealing. Hide glue and starch pastes work for paper, wood, and cloth.

## Techniques
- **Clean and roughen** surfaces first — grease and gloss defeat every glue.
- **Mechanical beats chemical for loads:** a bolt, lashing, splint, or stitched
  seam carries force a glue joint alone will not. Combine them — glue *and* clamp,
  patch *and* stitch.
- **Waterproofing:** seal stitched seams with seam sealer, wax, or pitch; a patch
  on rain gear needs its edges sealed or it wicks water through the needle holes.
- **Splinting a broken shaft/pole:** sandwich the break between rigid splints
  (sticks, tent stakes) and lash tightly above and below the break.
- **Threads and fasteners:** a stripped screw hole re-bites with a matchstick or
  toothpick glued in; wire or a zip tie replaces a broken buckle or clip.

## Safety
Many glues and solvents are flammable and give off harmful fumes — **use with
ventilation and away from flame**, and keep them off skin and eyes. Let repairs
cure fully before loading them.
