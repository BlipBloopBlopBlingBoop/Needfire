# Controlling Severe Bleeding

Severe bleeding can be fatal in minutes. Act immediately. Protect yourself from
blood contact if possible (gloves, a barrier), but do not delay for a serious
arterial bleed.

## Steps
1. **Apply firm direct pressure** on the wound with a clean cloth or your hand.
   Press hard and do not release to "check" — keep pressure constant.
2. **Pack and press** deep wounds: pack gauze or clean cloth firmly into the
   wound, then maintain pressure on top.
3. **Elevate** the injured limb above the heart if no fracture is suspected.
4. If bleeding from a limb does not stop with pressure and is life-threatening,
   **apply a tourniquet** 5–7 cm (2–3 in) above the wound (not on a joint).
   Tighten until bleeding stops. **Note the time applied.** Do not loosen it.
5. Keep the person warm and lying down; treat for shock. Reassure them.

## Signs of shock (from blood loss)
Pale, cold, clammy skin; rapid weak pulse; rapid breathing; confusion or
anxiety; thirst. Lay them flat, raise the legs if no spinal/leg injury, keep
them warm, and keep monitoring.

## After bleeding is controlled
Do not remove a soaked dressing — add more on top. Keep the wound covered and the
person still. Seek the highest level of medical care available.

> reach professional help or a trained responder, do so.
