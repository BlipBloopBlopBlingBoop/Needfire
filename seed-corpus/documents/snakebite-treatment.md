# Snakebite Treatment

Move the person away from the snake and treat every bite as venomous. Panic and
exertion pump venom through the body faster — your first medicine is calm.

## Steps
1. **Keep the victim calm and still.** Have them sit or lie down. Carry them if
   possible rather than letting them walk.
2. **Immobilize the bitten limb** with a splint or sling and keep it at about
   **heart level**.
3. **Remove rings, watches, and tight clothing now**, before swelling makes it
   impossible.
4. **Mark the edge of the swelling** with a pen and **write the time** next to
   it. Repeat every 15–30 minutes to track spread.
5. Wash the bite gently and cover with a clean dressing.

## Do NOT
**Do not cut the wound, suck out venom, apply a tourniquet, apply ice, or give
alcohol.** All of these worsen the injury or speed the venom, and none remove
it.

## Pressure-immobilization bandage — neurotoxic snakes only
A firm wrap of the whole limb (snug as for a sprain) plus a splint slows
paralysing venom. Use it **only for neurotoxic snakes** — Australian elapids,
coral snakes, and similar. **Do not use it for vipers or rattlesnakes**: their
venom destroys tissue, and trapping it in the limb concentrates that damage.

## Get to antivenom
**Antivenom is the only definitive treatment.** Evacuate to medical care even
if symptoms seem mild — envenomation can worsen over hours. Note the snake's
colour, pattern, and size **from a safe distance** (a photo is ideal); do not
try to catch or kill it. Some bites inject no venom ("dry bites"), but **never
assume you got one**.

> General reference only, not a substitute for trained medical care. If you can
> reach professional help or a trained responder, do so.
