# Sprains, Strains, and Joint Injuries

A **sprain** is a stretched or torn ligament (joint), a **strain** a stretched or
torn muscle/tendon. Both cause pain, swelling, and weakness. Most heal with rest
and support; the job in the field is to control swelling, protect the part, and
tell a sprain from a fracture.

## Sprain/strain vs. fracture
Suspect a **fracture** (see *Fractures and Splinting*) and treat as one if there
is: obvious deformity, a bone tender at one precise point, a snap heard at injury,
inability to bear any weight or use the limb at all, or numbness/cold/pale beyond
the injury. When in doubt, splint and treat as a fracture.

## First aid — POLICE / RICE
For the first 24–72 hours:
- **P — Protect** the joint from further injury.
- **OL — Optimal Loading:** rest it early, but **gentle, pain-free movement and
  use as it settles** heals better than total immobility. (The older advice was
  strict **Rest**.)
- **I — Ice:** a cold pack (wrapped, not on bare skin) for **15–20 minutes every
  2–3 hours** for the first day or two reduces pain and swelling.
- **C — Compression:** an elastic wrap, firm but **not so tight it causes
  numbness, tingling, or cold/blue toes/fingers** beyond the wrap. Loosen if it
  does.
- **E — Elevation:** raise the injured part above heart level when you can, to
  drain swelling.

Simple pain relief helps; NSAIDs (ibuprofen) reduce swelling if not contraindicated.

## Avoid early on (first 48–72 h)
The old **"HARM"** factors worsen swelling: **Heat**, **Alcohol**, **Running/hard
use**, and **Massage** of the fresh injury.

## Dislocations
A joint visibly out of place (shoulder, finger, kneecap) is a **dislocation**.
- **Do not force it back** unless you are trained and help is far off — you can
  worsen the damage. Support it in the most comfortable position, ice, and get help.
- **Check circulation and sensation** beyond the joint; a dislocation pressing on
  vessels or nerves (pale, cold, numb) is more urgent.

## When to seek help
Can't bear weight or use the limb, severe or increasing pain/swelling, deformity,
numbness/coldness, a joint that won't move, or no improvement over a few days.
