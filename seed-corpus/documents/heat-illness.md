# Heat Illness — Exhaustion and Heat Stroke

Heat illness is a spectrum: **cramps → exhaustion → stroke**. Catch it early
and it is easily reversed; miss the turn to heat stroke and it kills. The
dividing line is the brain: **confusion means heat stroke**.

## Heat exhaustion
Heavy sweating, weakness, nausea, headache, dizziness, pale clammy skin —
but thinking clearly.
1. **Stop all work.** Move to shade and loosen or remove clothing.
2. Lie flat with **legs raised**.
3. **Sip water or oral rehydration solution** steadily.
4. Cool with a **wet cloth on the skin plus fanning**.
Recovery should come within 30 minutes. If it does not, or the mind clouds,
treat as heat stroke.

## Heat STROKE — life-threatening
Confusion, strange behaviour, stumbling, seizure, or collapse in the heat, with
**hot skin — which may be dry OR still sweaty**.
**Cool aggressively NOW. Cool first, transport second.**
- **Whole-body immersion in cool water** (stream, trough, tub) is the best
  treatment — keep the head supported above water.
- Otherwise, **douse continuously** with water and fan hard, and put **cold
  packs at the neck, armpits, and groin**.
- Stop active cooling once they become alert. **Shivering alone is not a reason
  to stop** — heat stroke victims often shiver while still dangerously hot.
  Give nothing by mouth while confused.

## Prevention
- **Acclimatize over 1–2 weeks** before heavy work in heat; increase workload
  gradually.
- Schedule hard work for **dawn and dusk**.
- Drink about **0.5–1 litre per hour** during hard work in heat, **with
  electrolytes or salted food** — plain water alone for hours risks
  dangerously low sodium.
- **Check urine colour**: pale straw is right; dark means drink more.

> reach professional help or a trained responder, do so.
