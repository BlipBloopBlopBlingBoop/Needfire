# Finding Water in the Field

A person can survive only about three days without water. Prioritize finding and
purifying it early, before dehydration impairs judgement.

## Where to look
- Valleys and low ground where water collects; the base of cliffs; green
  vegetation lines that stand out from drier surroundings.
- Follow animal tracks, insect swarms (especially bees, which stay near water),
  and converging game trails downhill.
- Dew: drag a clean cloth through morning grass and wring it out.
- Rain: rig a tarp or large leaves to funnel rainfall into a container.

## Collecting from plants and ground
- Transpiration bag: tie a clear plastic bag over a leafy, non-toxic branch in
  sun; water vapour condenses inside over hours.
- Solar still: dig a pit, place a container at the bottom, cover with plastic
  weighted in the centre so condensation drips into the container.

## Cautions
- Assume all wild water needs purification (see Water Purification Methods).
- Avoid water near industrial sites, mines, or heavy agriculture — purification
  does not remove chemicals or heavy metals.
- Do not drink seawater or urine; they worsen dehydration.
- Stagnant, foul-smelling, or discoloured water is a last resort even after
  treatment.

> General reference only, not professional advice. Treat all wild water as
> unsafe until purified — see *Water Purification Methods*.
