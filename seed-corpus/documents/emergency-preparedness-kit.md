# Emergency Preparedness and the Go-Bag

Preparation done calmly in advance is worth more than any heroics during a crisis.
The aim is to be able to **shelter in place** for a stretch, and to **leave fast**
with the essentials if you must. Build to your real risks (flood, fire,
earthquake, storm, outage) and your household.

## The Rule-of-Threes shopping list
Stock to cover the survival priorities (see *Survival Priorities*):
- **Water:** ~**4 litres (1 gallon) per person per day** stored; a means to
  **purify** more (filter, bleach, the ability to boil). See *Rationing Food and
  Water*.
- **Food:** several days to weeks of **no-cook, long-life** food you actually eat;
  a manual can opener; extra for children, pets, and special diets.
- **Warmth/shelter:** blankets, sleeping bags, sturdy clothing, a tarp, a way to
  make heat and light safely.
- **Health:** a **first-aid kit**, personal medicines (rotated, in date), copies of
  prescriptions, hygiene and sanitation supplies.

## The grab-and-go bag (72 hours+)
One packed bag per person by the door, ready to carry:
- Water and a filter/purification tablets; high-calorie no-cook food.
- Emergency blanket, poncho/rain shell, hat, gloves, spare socks.
- **Light** (headlamp + spare batteries), **fire** (lighter + waterproof matches),
  a **knife/multi-tool**, **cordage**, **duct tape**.
- **Radio** (battery/crank) for information; a **whistle** and signal mirror.
- First-aid kit and personal medicines; hand sanitiser, soap, toilet supplies.
- **Power:** power bank / small solar; charging cables.
- **Documents** (waterproof bag or USB): ID, insurance, medical info, contacts,
  cash in small notes.
- Map and compass of your area; a pen and notebook.

## Plans matter as much as gear
- **Communication plan:** an out-of-area contact everyone checks in with (local
  lines jam first), and a written contact list — don't rely on a phone's memory.
- **Meeting points:** one near home, one outside the neighbourhood.
- **Evacuation:** know two ways out; keep vehicles fuelled above half; plan for
  those who need help and for pets/livestock.
- **Utilities:** know how to shut off water, gas, and electricity safely.
- **Documents and skills:** back up records; practise using your kit *before* you
  need it — an unfamiliar tool in a crisis is nearly useless.

## Maintain it
Check the kit on a schedule: **rotate water, food, batteries, and medicines**
before they expire, adjust clothing for the season, and update documents and plans
as your household changes. A kit built once and forgotten fails when opened.

> General reference only. Tailor supplies and plans to your household's needs,
> local hazards, and any official guidance for your area.
