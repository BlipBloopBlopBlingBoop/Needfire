# Lightning and Severe-Storm Safety

Lightning kills and maims people who thought the storm was still far away.
There is **no safe place outdoors** in a thunderstorm; the goal is to be inside
substantial shelter before the strike zone reaches you, and to stay there until
well after it passes.

## Judging how close it is — flash-to-bang
Light arrives instantly; sound travels about **3 seconds per kilometre**
(5 seconds per mile). Count the seconds between the **flash** and the **bang**,
then divide:
- Seconds ÷ 3 = distance in kilometres (seconds ÷ 5 = miles).
- 9 seconds ≈ 3 km; 15 seconds ≈ 5 km; 30 seconds ≈ 10 km.

## The 30-30 rule
- **If the flash-to-bang gap is 30 seconds or less (≈10 km / 6 mi), the storm
  is close enough to strike you — go to shelter now.** Lightning routinely jumps
  10 km ahead of the rain ("a bolt from the blue").
- **Wait a full 30 minutes after the last thunder** before going back out. Most
  lightning deaths happen at the storm's leading and trailing edges, when it
  looks clear.

## Where to shelter
Best: a **large enclosed building** with wiring and plumbing, or a **hard-topped
metal vehicle** (windows up — the metal shell, not the tyres, protects you).
Once inside, stay off corded phones, and away from plumbing, wiring, and
windows; a nearby strike can travel through them.

## Caught in the open with no shelter
There is no safe option, only less-bad ones:
- **Get off high ground; avoid lone trees, poles, fences, and water.** Do not be
  the tallest thing around.
- Spread a group out so a single strike cannot hurt everyone.
- Put insulation (a pack, a foam pad) under you; **crouch low on the balls of
  your feet, heels together, head down** to minimise ground contact. Do not lie
  flat — ground current spreads through the earth from a strike.
- A dry ditch or low spot beats a hilltop.

## After a strike
A lightning victim carries no charge — **it is safe to touch them at once.**
Many have their heart or breathing stopped and can be saved: check breathing and
**start CPR immediately** if absent. Treat burns and hidden injuries and get help.

> General reference only, not a substitute for trained professional care. If you
> can reach professional help or a trained responder, do so.
