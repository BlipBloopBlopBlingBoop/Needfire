# Head Injury and Concussion

Any real blow to the head can injure the brain. Most are mild concussions that
recover, but a minority bleed inside the skull and deteriorate over minutes to
hours — so **watch anyone with a head injury closely**, and know the danger signs
that mean urgent help.

## Concussion (mild traumatic brain injury)
Signs after a knock to the head, with or without brief loss of consciousness:
- Dazed or confused, "seeing stars", headache, dizziness.
- Nausea, blurred vision, ringing ears, sensitivity to light/noise.
- Memory gaps around the event; slowed responses; unsteadiness.

**Care:** stop activity, rest physically and mentally, and stay with them. Most
symptoms ease over days. **Do not let them return to hard work, sport, or risk
of a second hit** until fully recovered — a second blow before healing can be far
more serious. Simple pain relief (paracetamol) is preferable to NSAIDs early,
which may raise bleeding risk.

## RED FLAGS — get advanced help now
Any of these after a head injury signals possible bleeding or serious injury:
- **Loss of consciousness**, or increasing **drowsiness / hard to wake**.
- **Repeated vomiting**, or a **worsening, severe headache**.
- **Confusion, agitation, or strange behaviour** that gets worse.
- **A seizure** (fit).
- **Unequal or unreactive pupils**, weakness/numbness in a limb, slurred speech,
  loss of coordination, or vision changes.
- **Clear or bloody fluid from the nose or ears**, or bruising around both eyes
  or behind the ears (signs of skull fracture).
- Any head injury in someone on **blood thinners**, an infant, or the elderly.

## What to do
1. If unresponsive: open the airway, check breathing (start CPR if absent), and
   **assume a neck/spine injury** — keep the head and neck still and aligned.
2. Control scalp bleeding with gentle direct pressure (do not press on a
   suspected skull fracture).
3. Keep them still, calm, and monitored; **wake and check them periodically** for
   the first 24–48 hours. Nothing to eat or drink if they may need surgery or are
   drowsy.
4. Deterioration over minutes/hours is the emergency — get help fast.
