# Burn Treatment

Stop the burning first: remove the person from the source, smother flames
(stop, drop, roll), cut power before touching an electrical casualty. Then cool
the burn — early cooling limits the damage.

## Steps
1. **Cool with cool running water for 20 minutes.** Not ice, not ice water —
   ice deepens the injury. Cooling helps even if started an hour after the burn.
2. **Remove rings, watches, and tight clothing early**, before swelling starts.
   Do not pull off clothing stuck to the burn.
3. **Cover** with a clean non-stick dressing or kitchen cling film laid loosely
   over the wound. Keep the person warm — large burns lose heat fast.
4. **Do NOT** pop blisters, or apply butter, toothpaste, oil, or grease. They
   trap heat and cause infection.

## How big is it?
The victim's own **palm (with fingers) is about 1% of their body surface**.
Count palms to estimate the burned area.

## When a burn is critical
Treat as life-threatening if any of these apply: burns to the **face, hands,
genitals**, or over joints; any sign of **airway burn** (singed nostrils,
hoarse voice, soot in mouth); more than **10% of body surface**;
**full-thickness** burns (painless, white, leathery, or charred); or **smoke
inhalation**. Evacuate to the highest care available.

## Special burns
- **Electrical**: damage runs deep between entry and exit wounds even when the
  skin looks minor. Watch for heart rhythm problems.
- **Chemical**: brush off dry powder first, then **flush with water for 20+
  minutes**. Remove contaminated clothing; protect yourself.

## Hydration
Large burns leak enormous amounts of fluid and cause shock. Give frequent sips
of water or oral rehydration solution to anyone with a large burn who is awake
and able to drink.
