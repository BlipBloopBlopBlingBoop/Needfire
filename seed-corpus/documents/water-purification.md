# Water Purification Methods

Unsafe water carries pathogens (bacteria, viruses, protozoa) and sometimes
chemical contamination. Purification targets pathogens; it does not remove most
chemicals or heavy metals.

## Boiling (most reliable for pathogens)
Bring water to a rolling boil for at least 1 minute. Above ~2,000 m (6,500 ft)
elevation, boil for 3 minutes because water boils at a lower temperature. Boiling
kills bacteria, viruses, and protozoa. Let it cool naturally; do not add ice.

## Filtration
A filter rated for 0.2 micron or finer removes bacteria and protozoa (including
Giardia and Cryptosporidium) but NOT viruses, which are smaller. Combine
filtration with a chemical or UV step in areas where viruses are a concern.

## Chemical disinfection
- Unscented household bleach (sodium hypochlorite, 5–6%): add 2 drops per litre
  of clear water (4 drops if cloudy), stir, wait 30 minutes. A faint chlorine
  smell should remain; if not, repeat the dose and wait another 15 minutes.
- Iodine and dedicated purification tablets work similarly — follow the package
  dose and contact time. Iodine is not recommended for pregnant people, those
  with thyroid problems, or long-term use.

## Clarify cloudy water first
Let sediment settle, then pour off the clear water, or filter through a clean
cloth, before disinfecting. Disinfectants work poorly in turbid water.

> This is general reference information, not professional advice. When in doubt,
> use more than one method (e.g. filter then boil).
